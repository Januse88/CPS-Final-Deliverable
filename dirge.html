The Dirge and the Second Line

One major source of creativity for many is past and present sadness. While tragedy is never something that a person should want, but once it is over it can become one of the greatest fonts of energy at a creative personâ€™s disposal.

I named this section after the guest lecture by Steve Prince. The idea is the procession that often follows a funeral in New Orleans. The procession will begin with a dirge, an expression of mourning and sadness, but then it will transition into the second line, which is a period of celebration of the life of the person who has passed. This is such an interesting dichotomy, and one that can be very jarring to the uninitiated.

Being able to channel sadness into something useful is a very powerful tool in the toolbelt of creativity. Everybody has experienced sadness, at least to a certain degree, and so it is a potential source that we all can draw from in order to advance our creativity.
